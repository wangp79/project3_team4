OUR LOCAL LANDSCAPE


The local space surrounding earth has a multitude of comets, asteroids and various detritus, which are frequently at risk of colliding the Earth. NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory has been

tracking these various objects and plotting their future trajectories. These NEO’s (Near Earth Objects) are potentially hazardous to terrestrial life to varying degrees, and also populate our local space region.

One astronomical unit (AU) is the distance between Earth and the Sun. This is around 150 million kilometres or 8.3 light minutes. A small solar system body is considered to be a NEO

if it travels within 1.3 astronomical units (AU) of the Sun. The more dangerous of these bodies are refered as Potentially Hazardous Asteroids (PHA). These are defined by their potential to make threatening close approaches to the Earth.
